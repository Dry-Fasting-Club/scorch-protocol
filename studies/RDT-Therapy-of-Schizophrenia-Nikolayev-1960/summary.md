# Study Summary: Relieving-Dietary Therapy of Schizophrenia and its Physiological Justification - Yuri Nikolayev (1960)

**Topic:** Clinical validation of RDT for severe psychiatric disorders.
**Key Focus:** Biological self-healing, suppression of "pathological excitement," and neural reset.
**Study Type:** Doctoral Dissertation / Massive Case Study (7,000+ patients)

## Overview
This 1960 dissertation is the scientifically definitive text that legitimized fasting within the USSR Ministry of Health. Dr. Yuri Nikolayev challenged the traditional psychiatric view that food refusal in mental patients was a symptom of disease to be countered with force-feeding. Instead, he hypothesized that it was an evolutionary **protective inhibition** mechanism. By allowing the patient to fast, the brain is relieved of the metabolic burden of digestion, allowing it to "reset" and resolve pathological neural firing patterns.

## Study Design & Methodology

### Clinical Context
- **Sample Size:** Documented results from over 7,000 patients treated between 1945 and 1960.
- **Patient Profile:** Majority were patients with "treatment-resistant" Schizophrenia (Catatonic, Paranoid, and Simple types), as well as those with severe depression and anxiety neuroses.
- **Intervention:** Prolonged water fasting (RDT) lasting 20–30 days, followed by a controlled 20–30 day recovery period.

### Physiological Assumptions
- **The "Natural Refusal" Hypothesis:** Nikolayev observed that animals and "madmen" naturally refuse food when in crisis. He categorized this as a biological self-preservation instinct (Natural RDT).
- **Protective Inhibition:** Based on Pavlovian Pavlovian theory, fasting induces a state of "rest" for the central nervous system, allowing for the repair of synaptic dysfunction.

## Main Findings

### 1. The Stages of Psychiatric Resolution
- **Stage of Excitation (Days 1-4):** Psychiatric symptoms may temporarily intensify as the body struggles with hunger.
- **The Turning Point (Acidotic Crisis):** Around Days 7-10, the "Acidotic Crisis" occurs. Psychologically, this is when the "clouding of consciousness" begins to lift.
- **Compensated Stage:** Deep remission begins. Catatonic patients often start speaking; paranoid ideation loses its emotional "charge."

### 2. Clinical Outcomes
- **Significant Improvement:** Approximately 65-75% of patients showed marked clinical improvement.
- **Remission Stability:** A subset of patients previously hospitalized for years were able to return to society and work.
- **Physical Side Effects:** Improvement in cardiovascular efficiency, resolution of chronic skin issues, and normalized metabolic markers.

### 3. Neural "Re-wiring"
Nikolayev proved that fasting stimulates **neuroplasticity** (though not named as such in 1960). By clearing metabolic byproducts and reducing systemic inflammation, the "pathological focus" in the brain dissipated.

## Conclusions & Recommendations

1. **Fasting is a Biological Imperative:** For organisms in deep physiological or psychological shock, fasting is the primary recovery mechanism.
2. **Beyond Psychiatry:** While focused on Schizophrenia, Nikolayev noted that the "physiological justification" applied to all psychosomatic illnesses where the brain-body feedback loop is broken.
3. **Medical Supervision Requirement:** Due to the severity of psychiatric symptoms during the first 10 days, Nikolayev insisted on hospital-based supervision for these cases.

### Practical Guidelines
- **The "Switch" Mechanism:** The shift from external to internal nutrition is the trigger for the brain reset.
- **Persistence:** Nikolayev cautioned that stopping a fast before the acidotic crisis often leads to incomplete psychological resolution.
