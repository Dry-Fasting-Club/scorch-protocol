# Study Summary: Problems of Therapeutic Fasting - Nikolayev & Anokhin (1969)

**Topic:** Scientific and clinical validation of therapeutic fasting (RDT).
**Key Focus:** Central nervous system (CNS) dynamics, biochemical adaptation, and official clinical guidelines.
**Study Type:** Collection of Scientific Papers / Experimental & Clinical Studies

## Overview
"Problems of Therapeutic Fasting" (Проблемы лечебного голодания) is a comprehensive 1969 volume co-edited by Professor Yuri Nikolayev and Academician P.K. Anokhin, a world-renowned neurophysiologist. This text represents the height of Soviet fasting science, bringing together researchers from across the USSR to prove that prolonged fasting is a rigorous medical intervention rather than "starvation." It provided the data required for the USSR Ministry of Health to issue official directives for the use of fasting in state hospitals.

## Study Design & Methodology

### Contributors
- **Leading Academicians:** Included P.K. Anokhin (Functional Systems Theory), biochemical researchers, and clinical psychiatrists.
- **Data Scope:** Analysis of over 10,000 clinical cases and corresponding lab work (blood chemistry, EEG, hormone panels).

### Focus Areas
1. **Neurophysiology:** How the brain signals hunger and how it switches to a "protected" state during fasting.
2. **Biochemistry:** Detailed mapping of acid-base balance, ketone production, and protein-sparing mechanisms.
3. **Clinical Diagnostics:** Identifying the signs of a "successful" fast vs. one that should be terminated.

## Main Findings

### 1. Functional Systems Theory (Anokhin)
Fasting is described as the ultimate test of the body's **Functional Systems**. Anokhin proved that under fasting conditions, the body reorganizes its internal resources with surgical precision, prioritizing high-order neural and cardiovascular functions while scavenging "biological waste" for fuel.

### 2. The Acidotic Crisis & Homeostasis
- **Biochemical Shift:** The study mapped the exact pH drop in the blood during the first 6-8 days.
- **Adaptation:** It proved that the "crisis" is not a failure of homeostasis but a **refining** of it. Once the crisis clears, the body operates with greater metabolic efficiency than it did *before* the fast.

### 3. Complications and Safety
- **Objective Safety:** The volume addressed fears of "organ failure." It proved that in the absence of absolute contraindications (e.g., Type 1 Diabetes, Stage 4 Cachexia), the body is genetically programmed to handle 30+ days of fasting without permanent damage.
- **Side Effects:** Headaches, nausea, and tachycardia were documented as transient responses to toxin mobilization, manageable via the "Hygienic RDT" (enemas, walking, air).

## Clinical Guidelines (Official USSR Standards)
The 1969 volume established the protocols still used in Russian clinics today:
- **Duration:** 14-25 days for maximum therapeutic yield in chronic disease.
- **Monitoring:** Daily tracking of weight, pulse, blood pressure, and tongue coating.
- **The "Recovery Formula":** Established the 1:1 ratio—refeding must be equal in length to the fast.

## Conclusions & Recommendations

1. **RDT is a Systemic Reset:** It is not merely a "diet" but a profound physiological reset that affects every cellular process.
2. **Neuro-Endocrine Balance:** Fasting was found to normalize cortisol and adrenalin levels in chronically stressed individuals.
3. **Legitimacy:** This text definitively classified RDT as a legitimate medical procedure, moving it from "fringe" practice to state-endorsed medicine.

### Practical Guidelines
- **The EEG Shift:** EEG readings often normalized during the fast, showing a reduction in "background noise" in the brain.
- **Protein Sparing:** Data confirmed that the body loses very little functional muscle mass compared to fat and pathological tissue (autophagy).
