# Study Summary: Anthropometric, Hemodynamic, Metabolic, and Renal Responses during 5 Days of Food and Water Deprivation

**Authors:** Ioannis A. Papagiannopoulos, Vassilis I. Sideris, Michael Boschmann, Olga S. Koutsoni, Eleni N. Dotsika  
**Topic:** Comprehensive analysis of body composition changes, kidney function, and vital parameters during 5-day dry fasting  
**Study Type:** Clinical intervention study with daily monitoring  
**Published:** Forschende Komplementärmedizin / Research in Complementary Medicine (2013)

## Overview
This landmark Greek study provides the first systematic daily documentation of anthropometric, hemodynamic, metabolic, and renal responses during extended food and water deprivation (FWD). The research reveals dramatic improvements in kidney function (up to 67% increase in GFR), massive abdominal volume reduction, and introduces novel body measurement parameters. The findings challenge conventional assumptions about "visceral fat" and suggest that much of central obesity may actually be tissue edema rather than adipose tissue.

## Study Design & Methodology

### Participants
- **Total Sample:** 10 healthy volunteers
- **Demographics:** 4 men, 6 women
- **Age Range:** 19-66 years (mean 47.5 years)
- **BMI Range:** 19-38 kg/m² (mean 28 kg/m²)
- **Study Date:** December 2007
- **Setting:** Outpatient monitoring with daily clinical examinations

### Intervention Protocol
**Pre-Intervention (2 days):**
- Normal eating according to daily habits
- Baseline measurements established

**Intervention (5 days):**
- **Complete abstinence** from all food and water
- Daily walking 30-60 minutes
- Normal daily activities at moderate intensity
- Sufficient sleep

**Post-Intervention Refeeding (3 days):**
- **Day 6:** 3 glasses (250 mL) juice + fruit
- **Day 7:** Juice + light meal
- **Day 8:** Juice + regular meal
- **Critical Protocol:** Chew each bite at least 20 times, drink juice with teaspoon

### Measurement Parameters

**Anthropometric Measurements:**
- Weight (daily)
- 6 body circumferences:
  - Neck circumference (NC) at cricothyroid membrane
  - Chest circumference at axilla (CCA)
  - Chest circumference at nipples (CCN)
  - Waist circumference (WC) under 12th rib
  - Hip circumference (HC) at gluteal furrow
  - **Oblique hip circumference (HCO)** - novel parameter from gluteal furrow to navel

**Novel Quotients Introduced:**
- QNC, QCCA, QCCN, QWC, QHC, QHCO
- Definition: Circumference decrease per kg of weight loss
- Purpose: Quantify volume-to-weight changes for each body region

**Hemodynamic Monitoring:**
- Systolic and diastolic blood pressure (SBP/DBP)
- Heart rate (HR)
- Hemoglobin oxygen saturation (SatO₂)
- Daily cardiac auscultation

**Metabolic Markers:**
- Serum glucose
- Serum osmolality
- Electrolytes: Na⁺, K⁺, Cl⁻
- Urea, creatinine
- Urine creatinine, volume, specific gravity

**Renal Function:**
- 24-hour creatinine clearance (GFR estimate)
- Urine output and osmolality

## Main Findings

### Weight Loss & Body Composition Changes

**Daily Weight Loss:**
- **Mean:** 1,390 ± 60 g/day
- **Total Loss (5 days):** ~6.95 kg (15.3 lbs)
- **Efficiency:** 50-100% greater than juice or water fasting

**Weight Loss Components:**
- **Urine Output:** 730 ± 80 g/day (53% of weight loss)
- **Stool Output:** 65 g/day (5% - mostly days 1-3)
- **Insensible Loss + CO₂:** 595 g/day (42%)
  - Calculation: 1,390g - 730g - 65g = 595g
  - This includes perspiration, respiration, and expired CO₂

**Interpretation:** The dramatic 1.4 kg/day weight loss reveals dry fasting as the most potent weight reduction method available. Unlike wet fasting, the body cannot conserve energy—it must aggressively metabolize fat to produce survival water.

### Anthropometric Changes: The Edema Revelation

**Circumference Reductions (5 days):**
- **Waist (WC):** Most dramatic reduction (data suggests ~10-15 cm total)
- **Oblique Hip (HCO):** Substantial reduction in lower abdomen
- **Chest at Nipples (CCN):** Moderate reduction
- **Chest at Axilla (CCA):** Moderate reduction
- **Hip (HC):** Moderate reduction
- **Neck (NC):** Smallest reduction but still considerable for small area

**Volume-to-Weight Quotients:**
- All Q-values (QNC, QWC, QHC, QHCO, QCCA, QCCN) showed substantial circumference decrease per kg of weight lost
- Demonstrates that weight loss translates to real volume reduction in all measured body regions

**Critical Insight - The "Visceral Fat" Paradigm Shift:**

The study authors make a groundbreaking observation: The massive waist circumference reduction (representing huge abdominal volume decrease) within just 5 days cannot be explained by visceral fat loss alone. Given the total weight loss, urine output, and insensible water loss, **this volume decrease is primarily attributable to elimination of edema fluid from abdominal organs**.

**Implications:**
1. Terms like "visceral fat," "central obesity," and "abdominal adiposity" may require reconsideration
2. **Disease risks associated with increased waist circumference might relate more to organ edema than fat tissue**
3. This challenges the entire conceptual framework of metabolic syndrome etiology

**Refeeding Response (Day 8):**
- All circumferences except NC and HC increased 12-34% of total decrease
- Demonstrates rapid fluid reaccumulation
- Highlights importance of gradual refeeding protocol

### Renal Function: The Kidney Rejuvenation Effect

**Creatinine Clearance (GFR) Changes:**
- **Baseline (Day 0):** 150 mL/min
- **Day 1:** 229 mL/min (+53%)
- **Day 2:** 222 mL/min (+49%)
- **Day 3:** 181 mL/min (+21%)
- **Day 4:** 250 mL/min (+67% - peak improvement)
- **Day 5:** 207 mL/min (+38%)
- **Day 8 (post-refeeding):** 125 mL/min (-17% below baseline)

**Interpretation:** The sustained 21-67% increase in kidney filtration rate throughout dry fasting is unprecedented in fasting literature. This improvement was universal across all participants regardless of BMI or age.

**Serum Creatinine:**
- Baseline: ~0.8 mg/dL
- Day 5: 0.96 mg/dL (slight increase, statistically significant but clinically insignificant)
- Remained within normal range (0.42-1.0 mg/dL)

**Proposed Mechanism:**
1. **Increasing serum osmolality** creates osmotic gradient between tissue fluid and blood
2. Facilitates passage of excess tissue water into bloodstream
3. **Kidney tissue decongestion** - removal of edema from renal parenchyma
4. Improved renal microcirculation
5. Enhanced glomerular filtration capacity

**Clinical Significance:**
- Consistent with literature showing negative association between 24-hour urine volume and GFR
- Consistent with positive association between urine osmolality and GFR
- Suggests therapeutic potential for chronic kidney disease (CKD)
- Contradicts conventional "drink more water for kidney health" advice

### Metabolic Adaptation & Safety Parameters

**Glucose Dynamics:**
- **Baseline:** ~5.5 mmol/L (99 mg/dL)
- **Day 3:** 3.4 mmol/L (60 mg/dL) - lowest point (p = 0.007)
- **Days 4-5:** Gradual increase back toward normal
- **Mechanism:** 
  - Days 1-3: Glycogen depletion, glucose supply interruption
  - Days 4-5: Hormonal counter-regulation (gluconeogenesis, cortisol, growth hormone)

**Serum Osmolality:**
- **Baseline:** ~285 mOsm/L
- **Day 5:** 302 mOsm/L (gradual increase)
- **Normal Range:** 275-295 mOsm/L
- Mild hypertonicity but within safe physiological range

**Electrolyte Balance:**

**Sodium (Na⁺):**
- Baseline: ~140 mEq/L
- Day 5: 148 mEq/L (gradual increase)
- Normal range: 135-152 mEq/L
- Stable and safe

**Potassium (K⁺):**
- **Day 2:** 5.29 mEq/L (peak - unexplained temporary increase)
- One participant approached 7 mEq/L but remained asymptomatic
- No cardiac arrhythmias observed
- Normal range: 3.5-5.5 mEq/L

**Chloride (Cl⁻):**
- Gradual increase parallel to sodium
- Day 5: 105 mEq/L
- Normal range: 95-107 mEq/L

**Urea:**
- Baseline: ~3.5 mmol/L (~21 mg/dL)
- Day 5: 8.2 mmol/L (49 mg/dL)
- Normal range: 1.7-8 mmol/L
- Reflects increased protein catabolism for gluconeogenesis

### Hemodynamic Stability

**Blood Pressure (SBP/DBP):**
- Remained stable throughout 5 days
- No significant changes from baseline
- No orthostatic hypotension requiring intervention

**Heart Rate (HR):**
- Stable throughout intervention
- No significant deviations
- No arrhythmias detected on auscultation or pulse oximetry

**Oxygen Saturation (SatO₂):**
- Remained stable at normal levels
- No hypoxemia

**Interpretation:** Despite complete water deprivation, the cardiovascular system maintained remarkable stability through sophisticated hormonal counter-regulation (aldosterone, vasopressin, renin-angiotensin system).

### Clinical Symptoms & Tolerance

**Days 2-3 (Peak Symptoms):**
- **Fatigue:** 7 of 10 participants (70%)
- **Nausea:** 2 of 10 participants (20%)
- **Headache:** 1 of 10 participants (10%)
- **Muscle pains:** 3 of 10 participants (30%)
- **Relief:** All symptoms easily managed with rest or warm bath
- **Resolution:** Subsided within 24-36 hours

**Days 4-5:**
- **Thirst:** Controllable feeling in all participants
- **Dehydration Signs:** None observed clinically
- **Energy:** All participants reported intervals of stamina and euphoria

**Remarkable Finding:** No participant showed clinical signs of dehydration despite 5 days without water. This demonstrates the body's sophisticated water conservation and metabolic water production capabilities.

## Comparative Analysis: 5-Day vs. 3-Day Dry Fasting

| Parameter | 5-Day FWD (Greek Study) | 3-Day Dry Fast (Khoroshilov) | Advantage |
|-----------|------------------------|------------------------------|-----------|
| Daily Weight Loss | 1,390 g/day | 1,100 g/day | 5-day: +26% |
| Peak GFR Increase | +67% (Day 4) | -12% (Day 3 - adaptive) | 5-day: Renal improvement |
| Glucose Nadir | 60 mg/dL (Day 3) | Not specified | Similar adaptation |
| Max Osmolality | 302 mOsm/L (Day 5) | Not specified | 5-day: Higher stress |
| Dehydration Grade | None (clinical) | Mild (2.8% fluid loss) | 5-day: Better tolerance? |
| Waist Reduction | Massive (edema focus) | Not primary focus | 5-day: Unique insight |

**Key Distinction:** This Greek study extends observation to 5 days and focuses heavily on body composition and kidney function, revealing phenomena not visible in shorter 3-day protocols.

## Novel Contributions to Dry Fasting Science

### 1. The Edema-vs-Fat Paradigm Shift
- First study to systematically document that rapid waist circumference reduction during dry fasting is primarily **edema elimination**, not fat loss
- Challenges conventional understanding of "visceral adiposity"
- Suggests disease risks of central obesity may relate to chronic organ edema

### 2. The Kidney Rejuvenation Effect
- First documentation of sustained GFR increases up to 67% during extended dry fasting
- Universal effect across all participants
- Proposes decongestion mechanism: hypertonicity draws edema from kidney tissue → improved microcirculation → enhanced filtration

### 3. New Anthropometric Tools
- **Oblique Hip Circumference (HCO):** Novel measurement for lower abdominal volume
- **Q-Quotients:** Circumference-to-weight ratios as compliance and efficacy markers
- Provides standardized monitoring toolkit for dry fasting protocols

### 4. Extended Safety Data
- First systematic daily monitoring showing 5-day FWD is physiologically safe in healthy adults
- No dangerous electrolyte imbalances
- No cardiovascular instability
- No clinical dehydration despite zero water intake

## Mechanistic Insights

### The Hypertonicity-Edema Connection

**Proposed Cascade:**
1. **Water deprivation** → Serum osmolality increases (285 → 302 mOsm/L)
2. **Osmotic gradient** forms between tissue fluid and blood
3. **Tissue water mobilization** → Extra fluid passes from tissues to bloodstream
4. **Systemic decongestion:**
   - Abdominal organs shed edema → massive waist reduction
   - Kidney parenchyma decongestion → improved GFR
   - Intestinal wall/lumen dehydration → reduced abdominal volume, altered microbiome

### Why GFR Increases (Counter-Intuitive)

**Traditional Assumption:** Dehydration → reduced kidney blood flow → decreased GFR

**Dry Fasting Reality:**
1. Hypertonicity mobilizes chronic tissue edema
2. Kidneys chronically congested in overhydrated state
3. Dry fasting "wrings out" excess fluid from renal tissue
4. Microcirculation improves when interstitial pressure decreases
5. Glomeruli function more efficiently in decongested state

**Supporting Evidence:**
- Literature shows high urine volume and low osmolality are risk factors for faster CKD progression
- Fluid restriction improves CKD prognosis
- This study confirms the mechanism: osmotic concentration improves kidney tissue architecture

### Glucose Counter-Regulation

**Three-Phase Response:**
1. **Days 1-3:** Glycogen depletion → glucose drops to 60 mg/dL
2. **Hormonal Surge:** Cortisol, glucagon, growth hormone activate
3. **Days 4-5:** Gluconeogenesis ramps up → glucose stabilizes/rises

**This demonstrates the body's metabolic flexibility and survival programming for extended starvation.**

## Clinical Applications & Therapeutic Potential

### Obesity & Metabolic Syndrome
- **Most effective weight loss method:** 1.4 kg/day (50-100% faster than wet fasting)
- Rapid waist circumference reduction may lower CVD risk
- Improved insulin sensitivity (suggested by glucose dynamics)

### Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)
- **67% GFR improvement** suggests therapeutic potential
- May reverse early-stage kidney dysfunction
- Contradicts "hydration therapy" dogma for CKD

### Cardiovascular Disease
- Waist reduction without cardiovascular stress
- Stable blood pressure throughout
- May benefit hypertensive patients (suggested by hemodynamic stability)

### Inflammatory Conditions
- Massive edema elimination suggests anti-inflammatory effects
- Tissue decongestion may reduce inflammatory cascade
- Potential for autoimmune conditions

### Disease Risk Reduction
Evidence links circumference reductions to decreased risks of:
- **Neck circumference:** Sleep apnea, insulin resistance
- **Waist circumference:** Mortality, CVD, diabetes, metabolic syndrome, cancer, kidney stones
- **Waist-to-hip ratio:** Mortality, CVD, cancer

## Safety Considerations

### Demonstrated Safety (Under Study Conditions)
- **Population:** Healthy adults, normal kidney function
- **Sample Size:** Small (n=10) but intensive monitoring
- **Setting:** Daily physician examination
- **Outcome:** No serious adverse events

### Critical Success Factors
1. **Healthy baseline** kidney and cardiac function
2. **Daily medical monitoring** of vital signs and labs
3. **Controlled environment** (moderate activity, sufficient sleep)
4. **Gradual refeeding** protocol (critical for safety)

### Contraindications (Implied)
- Pre-existing kidney disease
- Cardiovascular instability
- Electrolyte disorders
- Pregnancy/lactation
- Extreme physical activity during fast

### The Potassium Question
- One participant approached dangerous K⁺ level (near 7 mEq/L)
- Remained asymptomatic with no arrhythmias
- **Recommendation:** Daily electrolyte monitoring essential for safety

## Limitations & Future Research Needs

### Study Limitations
1. **Small sample size** (n=10) - limits generalizability
2. **Short duration** - no data beyond 5 days
3. **Healthy participants only** - no data on diseased populations
4. **Missing data:** Ketone levels, hormonal profiles, oxidative stress markers
5. **No body composition analysis** (DEXA, BIA) to quantify fat vs. lean mass loss
6. **No long-term follow-up** - durability of improvements unknown

### Future Research Directions

**Hormonal Profiling:**

## Evolutionary & Historical Context

### Religious Fasting Traditions
- **Orthodox Christian Practice:** Monks and nuns practice FWD for several days during Easter fasting (1st century A.D. to present)
- **Byzantine Ascetics (6th-7th century):** Described spiritual clarification through FWD and warned against "polyposia" (excessive drinking)

### Ancient Medical Wisdom
- **Hippocrates (5th century B.C.):** Prescribed "oligoposia" (little drinking) and applied FWD for acute and chronic diseases
- Suggests ancient physicians observed therapeutic benefits empirically

### Modern Observations
- **Mount Athos Monks:** Study of exhaled air after 63 hours FWD detected volatile compounds (acetone, dichloromethane, toluene)
- Confirms ketosis and metabolic shifts during extended FWD

### Evolutionary Adaptation
- Humans evolved in water-scarce environments
- Natural selection favored sophisticated water conservation mechanisms:
  - Aldosterone secretion
  - ADH (vasopressin) concentration of urine
  - Metabolic water production from fat oxidation
  - Reduced perspiration under dehydration stress

**Modern Disconnect:** Western "8 glasses of water per day" advice contradicts evolutionary physiology and may chronically congest tissues.

## Conclusions & Key Takeaways

### Primary Findings

1. **Safety:** 5 days of complete food and water deprivation is physiologically safe in healthy adults under medical supervision

2. **Efficacy:** Most effective weight reduction method (1.4 kg/day), surpassing all conventional fasting protocols

3. **Kidney Rejuvenation:** Universal improvement in GFR (up to 67%) suggests therapeutic decongestion effect on renal tissue

4. **Edema Paradigm Shift:** Massive waist reduction primarily reflects organ edema elimination, not fat loss—challenges conventional "visceral adiposity" framework

5. **Metabolic Flexibility:** Body demonstrates sophisticated counter-regulation for hypoglycemia, hypovolemia, and hypertonicity

6. **Hemodynamic Stability:** No cardiovascular instability despite zero water intake, demonstrating evolutionary adaptation

### Practical Guidelines (Based on Study Protocol)

**Pre-Fasting:**
- Medical clearance with kidney function testing
- Baseline vital signs, weight, circumferences
- Baseline electrolytes, creatinine, glucose

**During 5-Day FWD:**
- **Activity:** Light walking 30-60 min/day, moderate daily activities
- **Rest:** Sufficient sleep, warm baths for symptom relief
- **Monitoring:** Daily weight, BP, HR, SatO₂
- **Labs:** Daily glucose, electrolytes, creatinine, urine output
- **Red Flags:** Severe symptoms, orthostatic hypotension, arrhythmias

**Post-Fasting Refeeding (CRITICAL):**
- **Day 6:** 3 glasses juice (250 mL each) + fruit
- **Day 7:** Juice + light meal
- **Day 8:** Juice + regular meal
- **Technique:** Chew each bite ≥20 times, sip juice with teaspoon
- **Rationale:** Prevent refeeding edema and digestive overload

### Clinical Recommendations

**Ideal Candidates:**
- Healthy adults with normal kidney function
- Metabolic syndrome seeking rapid improvement
- Early-stage CKD under medical supervision
- Obesity with central adiposity/edema

**Contraindications:**
- Advanced kidney disease (GFR <30 mL/min)
- Cardiovascular instability
- Electrolyte disorders
- Pregnancy, lactation, childhood
- Inability to tolerate medical monitoring

**Monitoring Requirements:**
- Daily physician examination
- Daily labs (minimum: glucose, electrolytes, creatinine)
- 24-hour urine collection for GFR calculation
- Vital signs twice daily

### The Bigger Picture

This study represents a landmark contribution to dry fasting science by:
1. Extending safe observation period to 5 days

5. Challenging fundamental assumptions about obesity pathophysiology

**The edema revelation may be the study's most significant legacy:** If disease risks attributed to "visceral fat" actually stem from chronic organ congestion, then dry fasting's unique ability to eliminate tissue edema positions it as a therapeutic intervention of profound importance for metabolic and cardiovascular disease.
