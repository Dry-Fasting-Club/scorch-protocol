# Study Summary: Fasting for Health (Голодание ради здоровья) - Yuri Nikolayev (1973)

**Topic:** Clinical methodology of Unloading-Dietary Therapy (RDT) and physiological adaptation stages.
**Key Focus:** The Acidotic Crisis, Internal Nutrition, and the Recovery Period.
**Study Type:** Clinical Monograph / Longitudinal Clinical Study

## Overview
Published in 1973, "Fasting for Health" is the foundational text for the Soviet/Russian clinical methodology of therapeutic fasting, known as **RDT** (*Razgruzochno-Dieticheskaya Terapiya*). Dr. Yuri Nikolayev, a psychiatrist, developed this method after observing the profound neurological and systemic healing effects of prolonged fasting on "incurable" patients. The study establishes fasting not as "starvation" but as a period of **internal (endogenous) nutrition** that resets the body's homeostatic mechanisms.

## Study Design & Methodology

### Clinical Context
- **Patient Population:** Over 8,000 patients treated over several decades at the Moscow Research Institute of Psychiatry.
- **Conditions Treated:** Schizophrenia, hypertension, bronchial asthma, rheumatoid arthritis, skin disorders (eczema), and metabolic syndrome.
- **Environment:** Supervised clinical setting with specific environmental controls (well-ventilated rooms, warm clothing to combat fasting-induced chilliness).

### The RDT Protocol (Wet/Water Fasting)
Nikolayev's classic protocol primarily used water fasting, though it provided the physiological blueprint for subsequent dry fasting research.
1. **Preparation:** 1-2 days of plant-based diet and a saline laxative.
2. **The Fasting Phase:** Drinking only water (distilled or spring) based on thirst.
3. **Daily Procedures:**
   - **Hygienic Enemas:** Daily or every other day to prevent autointoxication from stagnant fecal matter.
   - **Hydrotherapy:** Warm showers and baths to stimulate skin-based detoxification.
   - **Walking:** Mandatory 2-3 hours of outdoor walking to maintain metabolic activity and oxygenate the blood.
   - **Oral Care:** Tongue scraping and rinsing (mint/lemon water) to remove toxins excreted through the tongue.

## Main Findings: The Three Stages of Fasting

### Stage I: Food Excitation (Days 1–3)
- **Physiology:** Transition away from external glucose. Hunger is intense; the patient is irritable and focused on food.
- **Biochemistry:** Depletion of glycogen stores (liver and muscle).
- **Clinical Sign:** Salivation, stomach rumbling, and psychological restlessness.

### Stage II: Increasing Acidosis (Days 3–6)
- **Physiology:** The body begins breaking down fat (lipolysis), producing ketone bodies.
- **Symptoms:** Weakness, "acetone" breath, coated tongue (white/yellow), headaches, and dizziness.
- **The "Acidotic Crisis":** Usually occurs between **Days 6 and 9**.
  - **The Turning Point:** Once the acidosis reaches its peak, the body's buffer systems adapt and begin to "shuttle" ketones more efficiently.
  - **Subjective Shift:** Patients often feel a sudden surge of well-being, clarity, and energy immediately after the crisis passes. Hunger disappears completely.

### Stage III: Compensated Acidosis (Crisis to End)
- **Physiology:** The body settles into deep endogenous nutrition. It begins a "selective" breakdown of tissues—targeting old cells, tumors, adhesions, and cysts first (autophagy).
- **Clinical Sign:** Pulse and blood pressure stabilize at lower, more efficient levels. Mental clarity is at its highest.

## The Recovery Period (The Most Crucial Phase)
Nikolayev emphasized that **"Anyone can fast, but only a wise man can recover correctly."** The recovery period must be equal in length to the fast itself.

### The Refeeding Protocol
| Phase | Duration | Primary Food | Key Rule |
|-------|----------|--------------|----------|
| **Day 1** | 24 Hours | Diluted fresh fruit/vegetable juices (50/50 with water) | Small sips (50-100ml) every 2 hours |
| **Day 2-3**| 48 Hours | Undiluted juices, thin vegetable broths, or oat/rice water | Absolutely NO salt or fats |
| **Day 4-6**| 72 Hours | Small amounts of grated fruit/vegetables, curdled milk (kefir/yogurt) | Chew thoroughly until food is liquid |
| **Day 7+** | Ongoing | Boiled vegetables, whole grains | Reintroduce salt very gradually |

### Critical Warnings
1. **The Danger of Salt:** Reintroducing salt too early causes massive fluid retention (edema) and can lead to hypertensive spikes or kidney stress.
2. **The Danger of Overeating:** Early overconsumption of solid protein or fat can cause "intestinal volvulus" or acute pancreatic distress.

## Clinical Results & Effectiveness
- **Immediate Improvement:** 70% of patients with "treatment-resistant" chronic illnesses showed significant symptom reduction.
- **Long-term Results:** A follow-up study of psychiatric patients showed that 47% remained in stable remission 6 years later, provided they followed the "Post-RDT" lifestyle guidelines.
- **Weight Loss:** Average loss of 10-15% of body weight, with significant metabolic improvements (lipids, glucose).

## Conclusions & Recommendations

1. **Acidotic Crisis is Vital:** The therapeutic reset does not fully begin until the first acidotic crisis (Day 6-9 in water fasting, Day 2-3 in dry fasting) is cleared.
2. **Internal Surgeon:** Fasting acts as an "internal surgeon," breaking down pathological structures through controlled autolysis.
3. **Recovery is Logic:** The healing happens during the refeed; the fast is merely the preparation. Failure to follow the refeed protocol invalidates the fast and can be dangerous.
4. **Hygienic Support:** Enemas, walking, and skin-brushing are not optional; they are required to manage the toxin load mobilized by the fast.

### Practical Guidelines for Today
- **Dry Fasting Context:** Nikolayev's 7-day water fast results are often achieved in 2-3 days of dry fasting due to the "triple" acceleration of the acidotic crisis.
- **Mental Preparation:** Expect the Day 3-6 malaise; it is a sign that the "surgery" has begun.
- **Salt-Free Refeed:** Maintain a salt-free environment for the first 3-5 days of refeeding to ensure the TSH/Kidney rebound is managed safely.
