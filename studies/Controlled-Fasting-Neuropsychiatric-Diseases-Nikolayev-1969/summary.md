# Study Summary: Treatment of Neuropsychiatric Diseases with Controlled Fasting - Yuri Nikolayev (1969)

**Topic:** Clinical training manual for administering RDT.
**Key Focus:** Methodological precision, hospital-based protocols, and symptom management.
**Study Type:** Instructional-Methodological Letter / Clinical Protocol

## Overview
This 1969 document ("Лечение нервно-психических заболеваний дозированным голоданием") was issued as an official methodological letter by the RSFSR Ministry of Health and the Moscow Research Institute of Psychiatry. Unlike a textbook or a theoretical paper, this letter was designed as a **practical manual** for medical doctors. it contains the exact instructions on how to set up a "fasting ward," how to screen patients, and how to manage the day-to-day progression of a 20-30 day medical fast.

## Study Design & Methodology

### Clinical Setting
- **Hospital Requirements:** Must be a quiet, well-ventilated psychiatric or psychosomatic hospital ward.
- **Supervision:** Constant monitoring by a trained physician and nursing staff.
- **Integration:** The letter outlines how RDT replaces or complements conventional pharmacotherapy (e.g., insulin shock or early antipsychotics).

### The Three-Phase Protocol
1. **The Unloading Period (Preparation):** Diagnostic screening, psychological briefing, and clearing the digestive tract.
2. **The Fasting Period (The "Active" Phase):** Consuming only water based on thirst; strictly avoiding all caloric intake.
3. **The Recovery Period (The "Refeeding" Phase):** Precise schedule of reintroducing foods, starting with juices.

## Main Findings & Instructions

### 1. Patient Selection (Indications)
- High success was noted for **Schizophrenia (sluggish/simple)**, **Bipolar Disorder (depressive phase)**, and **Obsessive-Compulsive Disorder**.
- Also recommended for **Physiological Exhaustion** and **Psychosomatic Asthma**.

### 2. Daily Management Protocols
The letter dictates a rigorous daily schedule to manage the toxic burden:
- **Morning Enema:** Every single morning to prevent the reabsorption of metabolic waste from the colon.
- **Warm Bath/Shower:** Daily to stimulate toxin excretion through the skin.
- **Physical Activity:** Patients must walk 2-3 hours daily in the fresh air, regardless of "feeling weak" (to prevent metabolic stagnation).
- **Oral Hygiene:** Tongue scraping and rinsing with lemon/water several times a day.

### 3. Managing the Acidotic Crisis
- The manual describes the crisis (Day 7-10) as the "threshold of healing."
- Doctors are instructed NOT to stop the fast during symptoms of headache, coated tongue, or mild tachycardia, but rather to increase the patient's intake of oxygen (walking) and skin-cleansing (baths).

### 4. The Refeeding "Law"
The most emphasized section of the manual. Refeeding is described as a **bio-chemical reconstruction**.
- **No Salt:** Salt is strictly forbidden for the first several days to prevent "fasting-induced edema" (fluid retention).
- **Gradualism:** The manual provides a 10-day refeeding chart, starting with 50ml diluted juices and progressing slowly to solid food.

## Conclusions & Recommendations

1. **Methodology Over Duration:** The success of the fast depends more on following the enema/walking/refeeding protocols than on the sheer number of days fasted.
2. **Safety through Discipline:** The letter assures doctors that controlled fasting is safe if the protocols are strictly maintained, but "dangerous" if the refeeding phase is rushed.
3. **Training Requirement:** Doctors are urged to undergo specialized training before opening an RDT department, emphasizing the unique psychological management of fasting patients.

### Practical Guidelines
- **Acetone Monitoring:** Checking for "acetone breath" as a biomarker that the internal switch to fat-burning has occurred.
- **Crisis Relief:** If the acidotic crisis is severe, the manual suggests a "half-glass of warm water with a small amount of lemon" to ease the detoxification process.
