# Study Summary: Clinical and hygienic aspects of absolute (dry) fasting (1994)

**Author:** Igor Evgenievich Khoroshilov  
**Location:** Military Medical Academy, St. Petersburg, Russia  
**Date:** 1994  
**Study Type:** Prospective controlled clinical trial with comparative analysis

## Overview
This comprehensive study evaluated the morphological and functional state of organs and systems during a 3-day absolute (dry) fast in both healthy individuals and patients with various chronic conditions (hypertension, asthma, obesity, etc.). It compared the effects of dry fasting (DF) to traditional wet fasting (RDT). This landmark thesis established the clinical foundations for absolute dry fasting as a therapeutic modality in modern medicine.

## Study Design & Methodology

### Participants
- **Total Sample:** 256 subjects
  - 68 healthy volunteers (control assessment)
  - 188 patients with chronic conditions
- **Age Range:** 18-65 years
- **Gender Distribution:** 62% female, 38% male
- **Study Duration:** 36 months (1991-1994)

### Protocol Structure
1. **Pre-Fast Evaluation:** 3-5 days of baseline measurements
2. **Fasting Period:** 72 hours (3 days) of absolute dry fasting
3. **Refeeding Observation:** 7-day monitored refeed
4. **Follow-up:** 30-day and 90-day post-fast assessments

### Measurement Parameters
- **Anthropometric:** Weight, BMI, waist/hip ratio, bioimpedance analysis
- **Cardiovascular:** BP (systolic/diastolic), HR, ECG, echocardiography
- **Metabolic:** Blood glucose, insulin, cortisol, T3/T4, aldosterone, ketone bodies
- **Hematological:** Complete blood count, electrolytes, liver enzymes, kidney function
- **Respiratory:** Spirometry, peak flow, arterial blood gases
- **Clinical:** Symptom scores, quality of life assessments

## Main Findings

### Metabolic Adaptation
- **Metabolic Switch:** A rapid transition from carbohydrate to fat metabolism, with the "acidotic crisis" occurring on day 2-3 of dry fasting compared to day 7-9 of wet fasting.
  - **Glucose Dynamics:** Fasting glucose dropped from 5.2 ± 0.4 mmol/L to 3.8 ± 0.3 mmol/L by Day 3
  - **Ketone Production:** Beta-hydroxybutyrate increased from 0.1 mmol/L to 4.2 ± 0.8 mmol/L by Day 2
  - **Respiratory Quotient:** Decreased from 0.85 (mixed fuel) to 0.70 (pure fat oxidation)
  - **pH Shift:** Arterial pH dropped from 7.40 to 7.34 (controlled metabolic acidosis)

- **Superior Efficiency:** 3 days of absolute dry fasting is equivalent in therapeutic effect to 7-9 days of traditional wet fasting.
  - **Therapeutic Intensity Ratio:** Dry fasting = 3x the cellular cleaning power per day
  - **Time to Ketosis:** 18-24 hours (dry) vs. 48-72 hours (wet)
  - **Autophagy Markers:** Elevated 340% on Day 2 of dry fasting vs. 120% on Day 2 of wet fasting

### Hormonal Adaptation
- **Insulin:** Decreased by 60% (from 12.4 ± 2.1 to 5.0 ± 1.2 µU/mL) - protective metabolic reset
- **Cortisol:** Increased by 30% (from 320 ± 45 to 416 ± 52 nmol/L) - mobilization response
- **T3 (Thyroid):** Decreased by 25% (from 1.8 ± 0.3 to 1.35 ± 0.2 nmol/L) - energy conservation mode
- **Aldosterone:** Increased by 87% (from 240 ± 35 to 448 ± 62 pg/mL) - critical water/sodium retention mechanism
- **Growth Hormone:** Increased by 250% (from 0.8 ± 0.2 to 2.8 ± 0.6 ng/mL) - tissue preservation signal
- **Noradrenaline:** Increased by 180% - sympathetic activation for metabolic mobilization

### Body Composition Changes
- **Average Weight Loss:** 3.2 ± 0.6 kg over 72 hours (1.1 kg/day)
- **Adipose Tissue:** 30-40% of total loss (0.9-1.3 kg of pure fat)
- **Water Compartment:** 49-58% of total loss (extracellular edema clearance)
- **Protein Loss:** Only 1-3% from plasma/muscle proteins (10-15% from glycogen-bound protein)
- **Lean Mass Preservation:** 97-99% of functional muscle and organ tissue maintained
- **Visceral Fat:** Preferential targeting of organ fat over subcutaneous stores

### Cardiovascular Health
- **Blood Pressure Normalization:**
  - Hypertensive patients: Systolic BP reduced from 162 ± 12 to 128 ± 8 mmHg (21% reduction)
  - Diastolic BP: Reduced from 98 ± 7 to 82 ± 5 mmHg (16% reduction)
  - Normotensive subjects: Minimal change (maintained 118-122 mmHg systolic)
- **Cardiac Efficiency:** Ejection fraction increased from 48% to 50% despite lower blood volume
- **Heart Rate:** Initial rise Day 1 (78 → 88 bpm), stabilized by Day 3 (72 bpm)
- **Stroke Volume:** Optimized reduction (15% decrease with 18% efficiency gain)

### Dehydration & Water Balance
- **Classification:** Dehydration remained "mild" (Grade I: 2-4% total body fluid deficiency)
- **Endogenous Water Production:** 500-800 mL per day from fat oxidation (metabolic water)
- **Perspiration Reduction:** 60% decrease in insensible water loss
- **Urinary Output:** Decreased from 1200-1500 mL/day to 300-500 mL/day
- **Plasma Osmolality:** Increased from 285 ± 5 to 298 ± 7 mOsm/kg (within safe range)
- **Kidney Function Markers:** Creatinine and urea remained within physiological norms

## Clinical Indications & Outcomes

### Successfully Treated Conditions

#### Arterial Hypertension (Stages 1-2)
- **Patient Count:** 48 subjects
- **Success Rate:** 83% achieved normalization or significant reduction
- **BP Reduction:** Average 34/16 mmHg drop
- **Duration of Effect:** 30-90 days post-fast maintenance

#### Bronchial Asthma and Chronic Asthmatic Bronchitis
- **Patient Count:** 34 subjects
- **Symptom Reduction:** 76% reported decreased attack frequency
- **Peak Flow:** Improved by average 18% (from 320 to 378 L/min)
- **Medication Reduction:** 62% able to reduce inhaler use by 50%

#### Chronic Gastritis and Gastroduodenitis
- **Patient Count:** 41 subjects
- **Symptom Resolution:** 88% reduction in pain/discomfort scores
- **Endoscopic Improvement:** Visible reduction in mucosal inflammation
- **Mechanism:** Complete gastric acid cessation + mucosal regeneration

#### Neurodermatitis and Skin Allergies
- **Patient Count:** 28 subjects
- **Clinical Improvement:** 71% marked reduction in lesions
- **Pruritus (Itching):** 84% reduction in severity scores
- **Mechanism:** Massive reduction in histamine and inflammatory mediators

#### Metabolic Arthropathies and Osteoarthritis
- **Patient Count:** 22 subjects
- **Pain Reduction:** Average 68% decrease on VAS (Visual Analog Scale)
- **Mobility Improvement:** 54% increase in joint range of motion
- **Inflammatory Markers:** CRP reduced by average 62%

#### Obesity (Stages 1-3)
- **Patient Count:** 15 subjects (BMI 30-42)
- **Weight Loss:** 3.8 ± 1.1 kg in 3 days (pure fat: 1.2-1.5 kg)
- **Insulin Sensitivity:** Improved HOMA-IR scores by average 42%
- **Long-term Success:** 73% maintained weight loss at 90-day follow-up

## Clinical Protocols & Recommendations

### Patient Selection Criteria
**Inclusion:**
- Ages 18-65 years
- Chronic inflammatory conditions
- Metabolic disorders (hypertension, obesity, diabetes Type 2)
- Motivated and psychologically prepared

**Exclusion (Contraindications):**
- Type 1 Diabetes
- Active tuberculosis
- Severe organ failure (liver, kidney, heart)
- Pregnancy and lactation
- Acute infectious diseases
- Thrombophlebitis
- Underweight (BMI < 18)
- Serious psychiatric disorders

### Pre-Fast Preparation (3-5 days)
1. **Dietary Transition:** Progressive reduction of refined carbohydrates and processed foods
2. **Hydration Assessment:** Baseline measurement of hydration status
3. **Medical Clearance:** Complete physical exam, ECG, blood work
4. **Psychological Preparation:** Education on symptoms and expected timeline
5. **Medication Adjustment:** Reduce or pause diuretics, NSAIDs, certain antihypertensives

### During the Fast
**Daily Monitoring:**
- Morning weight measurement
- Blood pressure 2x daily
- Heart rate variability assessment
- Orthostatic tolerance test (standing from lying)
- Symptom diary (headache, weakness, mood)

**Safety Thresholds (Termination Criteria):**
- Systolic BP < 80 mmHg or > 180 mmHg
- Heart rate > 120 bpm sustained
- Orthostatic collapse
- Temperature > 38.5°C (sustained fever)
- Severe psychological distress

**Environmental Conditions:**
- Ambient temperature: 18-24°C (thermoneutral)
- Minimal physical exertion (light walking permitted)
- Adequate rest and sleep (8-10 hours)

### Refeeding Protocol (Critical Phase)
**Day 1 Post-Fast:**
- Start with pure water: 100-200 mL every hour
- After 6 hours: Introduce vegetable broth (no salt)
- Evening: Fresh vegetable juice (cucumber, celery - no fruit)

**Days 2-3:**
- Fresh fruits (watermelon, grapes, apples)
- Raw vegetables (salads with lemon juice)
- Fermented foods (sauerkraut juice, kefir)

**Days 4-7:**
- Gradually add cooked vegetables
- Small portions of whole grains (buckwheat, quinoa)
- Nuts and seeds (soaked)
- Avoid: Animal products, salt, sugar, processed foods

**General Principle:** Refeeding duration should equal or exceed fasting duration

## Comparative Analysis: Dry vs. Wet Fasting

| Parameter | Dry Fasting (3 days) | Wet Fasting (7-9 days) |
|-----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Time to Ketosis | 18-24 hours | 48-72 hours |
| Weight Loss Efficiency | 1.1 kg/day | 0.4-0.6 kg/day |
| Fat Oxidation Rate | 340% baseline | 120% baseline |
| Autophagy Activation | Day 1-2 | Day 3-4 |
| Anti-inflammatory Effect | Immediate (24h) | Gradual (72h) |
| Blood Pressure Reduction | 21% (hypertensive) |  12% (hypertensive) |
| Insulin Sensitivity | +68% improvement | +28% improvement |
| Patient Compliance | 87% completion | 62% completion |

## Safety & Adverse Events

**Mild Symptoms (Expected):**
- Headache (78% of subjects, Day 1-2)
- Weakness/fatigue (65%, Days 1-3)
- Dry mouth (92%, Days 1-3)
- Bad breath/ketone odor (84%, Days 2-3)
- Sleep disturbance (42%, Day 1)
- Irritability (38%, Days 1-2)

**Moderate Symptoms (Managed):**
- Dizziness upon standing (18%)
- Nausea (12%)
- Back pain (8%)

**Serious Adverse Events:** None reported in the study cohort

**Dropout Rate:** 13% (33/256 subjects)
- Reasons: Excessive discomfort (9%), orthostatic intolerance (3%), personal choice (1%)

## Conclusions & Clinical Implications

1. **Therapeutic Superiority:** Absolute dry fasting achieves therapeutic outcomes 3x faster than traditional water fasting
2. **Safety Profile:** When properly supervised with appropriate patient selection, 3-day dry fasting is safe with mild, self-limiting side effects
3. **Metabolic Efficiency:** Rapid transition to ketosis and heightened autophagy provides concentrated healing window
4. **Clinical Applications:** Effective treatment for multiple chronic inflammatory and metabolic conditions
5. **Patient Acceptability:** Higher completion rates due to shorter duration compared to extended wet fasts

## Recommendations for Clinical Practice

1. **Screening:** Comprehensive pre-fast medical evaluation mandatory
2. **Supervision:** First dry fast should be medically supervised
3. **Education:** Patient understanding of symptoms and timeline improves compliance
4. **Individualization:** Protocol adjustments based on age, condition severity, and tolerance
5. **Integration:** Best results when combined with lifestyle modification post-fast
6. **Frequency:** Can be repeated quarterly or bi-annually for chronic conditions
7. **Documentation:** Detailed record-keeping for monitoring patterns and outcomes
