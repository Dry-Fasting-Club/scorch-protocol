Deep dives into specific mechanisms, protocols, and findings related to Ivermectin in the context of the Scorch Protocol.

In this section:
  • 1. BBB Crossing, Insulin Resistance & Neuroprotection

Topic 1: Blood Brain Barrier (BBB) Penetration & Metabolic Gating

Summary: The Metabolic Connection

Key Concept: The therapeutic "magic" of Ivermectin in Long Covid likely lies not in direct CNS viral clearance, but in its ability to modulate Insulin Resistance (IR) and systemic metabolism via the Farnesoid X Receptor (FXR), which indirectly protects the Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB).

1. The Mechanics: BBB Penetration & Safety

Current pharmacokinetic models indicate that Ivermectin has modest BBB penetration (typically 5â€“10% in CSF) under normal conditions. This is a safety feature, preventing neurotoxicity in most patients.

  • Baseline: In healthy BBB states, P-glycoprotein (P-gp) efflux pumps actively keep Ivermectin out of the CNS.
  • The Risk: Conditions that increase BBB permeability can allow "toxic" levels of Ivermectin to cross, leading to side effects like tics, dizziness, or paresthesia.

2. The Trigger: Free Fatty Acids (FFAs) & Leaky BBB

Systemic metabolic dysfunction, specifically Insulin Resistance (IR) and high circulating Free Fatty Acids (FFAs), directly compromises BBB integrity.

The Domino Effect: High Fat Diet / IR → Elevated FFAs → Endothelial Oxidative Stress → Downregulation of Claudin-5 (Tight Junctions) → Leaky BBB

Clinical Insight: Patients with untreated IR or those on high-fat diets (without metabolic flexibility) are at higher risk of "neurotoxic-like" side effects even at standard 12mg doses because their BBB is "leaky," mimicking an overdose state.

3. The Solution: Glucose Oxidation & T3 Sequencing

The core strategy to stabilize the BBB and make Ivermectin safer is to shift the body into a carb-burning state (glucose oxidation).

  • Protocol Nuance: Ivermectin often works best after T3 Therapy. T3 helps restart glucose oxidation and lowers serum FFAs, "pre-paving" the road for safe Ivermectin use.
  • The Keto Trap: Early disease states or strict ketogenic diets often have high circulating FFAs. If this is you, you may not react well to standard Ivermectin doses initially.
  • Use Low Dose: If you are in a high-FFA state, you can still benefit from low-dose protocols while your metabolism shifts. As healing progresses and glucose oxidation improves, tolerance to Ivermectin increases, allowing for deeper therapeutic work.

4. Protocol Integration & Risk Stratification

Turning Hurdle into Strategy

Pre-Screening: Assessing metabolic health (FFA levels, signs of IR) can predict Ivermectin tolerance.

Metabolic Support Strategy:

  • Focus on Bioenergetics: We prioritize supplements that generate energy (ATP) and support glucose oxidation.
  • Avoid AMPK Activators: In this specific protocol, we avoid fasting mimetics like Metformin and Berberine. We want to move away from the "hibernation/fat-burning" stress signal and towards a high-energy, pro-thyroid state.
  • Pulsed Dosing: Allows for clearance and minimizes accumulation risks while metabolic health improves.

Relevant Literature & Mechanisms

Supporting mechanisms from metabolic and pharmacological research:

  • Ivermectin as FXR Ligand: Jin et al. identified Ivermectin as a novel FXR ligand that improves insulin sensitivity and lowers serum glucose/cholesterol (Nature Communications, 2013).
  • FFAs and BBB Integrity: High plasma FFAs and oxidative stress are known to downregulate Claudin-5 and Occludin, leading to BBB hyperpermeability (Journal of Neuroinflammation; Frontiers in Cellular Neuroscience).
  • P-glycoprotein & Safety: Ivermectin is a P-gp substrate; its safety relies on an intact BBB. Inflammation (from high FFAs/IR) inhibits P-gp function, further increasing CNS exposure risks.
