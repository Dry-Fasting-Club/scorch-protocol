Phase 1: Preparation & Ketovore

Preparation is the foundation of a safe dry fast. Jumping into a dry fast from a high-carb, standard diet can lead to severe "keto flu" and unnecessary stress on the body. The goal here is to enter ketosis before the fast begins.

Readiness Checklist

Are you currently consuming caffeine? If YES → Taper off completely 1 week before. Withdrawal during a dry fast is agonizing.
Is your diet currently high in carbohydrates? If YES → Spend 3-7 days on a strict Ketogenic/Ketovore diet to prepare body with mild fat-adaption.
Are your bowels moving regularly? If NO → Consider a juice cleanse or enema right before the fast. You generally want an empty clean colon. The Scorch uses a coffee enema on the first day of the dry fast.

Medical & Safety Clearance

Recent Bloodwork Check Ensure: eGFR > 60 (Kidney), ALT/AST < 50 U/L (Liver), TSH 0.5-2.5 & Free T3 > 3.0 pg/mL (Thyroid).
No Acute Issues Must be free of: Active infections, flare-ups, pregnancy, heart disease, or use of diuretics.
Medication Safety DO NOT FAST if on liver-intensive medications (e.g., Benzos, Propranolol, Tylenol). Will need detox strategies first to clean liver.

The Metabolic Armor: Choosing Your Prep

Your preparation strategy defines the success of your fast. Choose the path that matches your metabolic health.

Option A: The Ketovore Protocol (Deep Healing)

Recommended for: Chronic illness (Long Covid, Autoimmune), limited fasting experience, or those who struggle with "keto flu".

⚠️ Warning ⚠️
This approach is not recommended for those that have already had a lot of fasting experience and lots of low-carb dieting experience and still deteriorating in health (will require slightly more advanced strategies).

This preparation is non-negotiable for deep repair. By eliminating carbohydrates and focusing on nutrient-dense animal fats and proteins, you lower inflammation and stabilize insulin before the fast begins. You prepare the body for keto and get the benefits of ketosis early, which makes the fasting safer and easier. It builds a metabolic armor, allowing you to slide into the dry fast without the crash, minimizing detox symptoms and maximizing resilience.

🧬 Why This Works: Quick Wins & The Long Game

1. Immediate Energy Rescue (Bypass the Blockage)
Your cells are currently struggling to burn glucose ("hypometabolism"). If we force them to fast without support, they crash. Ketones act as a backup generator, bypassing the broken glucose pathways and providing immediate, high-octane fuel (27% more ATP). This keeps your brain online while we prepare for the deep clean.

2. Pre-Fasting "Cleanup Crew"
Don't wait for the dry fast to start detoxing. Ketosis triggers a mild cleanup ("mitohormesis") now. By clearing out the easiest debris early, you reduce the shock of the actual fast. Think of it as taking out the trash before doing a deep spring clean.

3. Turning Down the Heat (Inflammation Control)
Entering a dry fast while inflamed is painful and dangerous. Ketones act as a biological fire extinguisher, inhibiting the inflammatory pathways (NLRP3) responsible for "cytokine storms." This calm baseline is essential for a safe fast.

4. Anxiety Armor (GABA Boost)
Fasting releases adrenaline. If you're already anxious, this can be overwhelming. Ketosis naturally boosts GABA (the brain's "chill" chemical), giving you the neurological stability to handle the stress of fasting without panic.

🔄 The Strategy: The Pivot
We are not staying Keto forever. This is a tactical intervention to survive the detox phase. We use fat adaptation to clear the debris and stabilize energy during the "crisis" of fasting. Once the slate is clean, we will pivot back. The post-fast goal is to reintroduce bioenergetic fuels (carbs) to rebuild a high-performance metabolism that can finally burn glucose efficiently again.

Option B: The Juicing Protocol (Light Prep)

Recommended for: Those with no severe autoimmune reactions to plants, good fasting experience, or those not afraid of a harder but stronger, fast.

A strategic juicing protocol can be used to hydrate and flood the body with enzymes. This method focuses on a "Moderate Carb Taper" to gently steady the insulin curve.

The "Diet Specs" below detail this lighter, moderate-carb approach.

Diet Specs

Carbohydrate Target 50g - 150g per day. Focus on calming insulin spikes, not eliminating them entirely yet.
Key Foods Juicing Focus, Fruit, Honey, Dairy (if tolerated), and Lean Proteins.
Strictly Avoid Grains, Sugars (refined), Seed Oils, and High-Oxalate Vegetables (Spinach, Almonds, Potatoes).
Duration Minimum 7 Days before the fast.

Liver & Colon Flushing

A clean colon is non-negotiable. When you dehydrate, any waste left in the colon can reabsorb toxins (autointoxication). Stagnant bile is also a major energy drain.

The Scorch Hack: First Day Coffee Enema

The Strategy Perform a Coffee Enema 12-14 hours INTO the dry fast.
Why?
  • Appetite Suppression: Cuts hunger signals immediately.
  • Bile Clearance: Forces the liver to dump toxic bile sludge.
  • Energy Boost: Caffeine absorbs directly via the portal vein.
  • Safety: Removes colon waste before deep dehydration sets in.

Hydration Loading & Electrolytes

Your pre-fast hydration strategy must account for the "electrolyte dump" that happens on low-carb diets. As insulin drops, kidneys flush out sodium and water, carrying other minerals with it.
