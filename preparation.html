Phase 1: Preparation & Ketovore

Preparation is the foundation of a safe dry fast. Jumping into a dry fast from a high-carb, standard diet can lead to severe "keto flu" and unnecessary stress on the body. The goal here is to enter ketosis before the fast begins.

Readiness Checklist

Are you currently consuming caffeine? If YES → Taper off completely 1 week before. Withdrawal during a dry fast is agonizing.
Is your diet currently high in carbohydrates? If YES → Spend 3-7 days on a strict Ketogenic/Ketovore diet to become fat-adapted.
Are your bowels moving regularly? If NO → Consider a juice cleanse or magnesium flush 2 days prior. You generally want an empty clean colon.

The Ketovore Approach

Yannick recommends a "Ketovore" approach—primarily animal-based with minimal low-toxicity plants if needed. This reduces gut inflammation and prepares the microbiome.

Important Caveat

Do you really need strict Ketovore? Strict Ketovore is primarily for severely chronically ill patients who react badly to fasting. If you are generally healthy or tolerate fasting well, you do not need to do a strict low-carb preparation.
  • Focus: Fatty meat, eggs, fish.
  • Avoid: Grains, sugars, seed oils, high-oxalate vegetables.
  • Duration: Minimum 3 days before the fast.

Hydration Loading

Before the fast starts, ensure you are well-hydrated with electrolytes. Do not "super-hydrate" to the point of flushing out minerals, but drink to thirst plus a little extra with quality sea salt.
