The Science of Growth Hormone & Immunity

Growth Hormone is not just for muscle growth; it is absolutely necessary for thymus regeneration, which is the seat of the immune system. For many people suffering from chronic illness or age-related immune decline, the thymus gland has involuted (shrunk) and replaced active tissue with fat.

hGH acts as a signal to re-awaken and regenerate the thymus, directly linking it to immune regeneration. Without adequate growth hormone signaling, the immune system may struggle to rebuild itself even after successful fasting.

The Starvation Effect (Rat Study) Is fasting always enough? → Not necessarily.

Research has shown that while fasting increases GH acutely during the fast, the long-term impact of severe caloric restriction or "starvation" can potentially suppress it afterwards if not managed correctly.

Key Study Finding: A study on rats demonstrated that starvation caused a massive drop—up to 50%—in growth hormone secretion that persisted for months afterwards. This suggests that for some individuals, especially those with depleted metabolic reserves, specific hGH support may be required to prevent this suppression and ensure regeneration continues.

GH's Role in Fat Mobilization During Fasting

While fasting acutely boosts GH secretion to support metabolic adaptation, GH does not primarily regulate blood glucose levels. Instead, its key function during energy restriction is to promote fat mobilization, facilitating the production of ketones as an alternative fuel source.

"Growth hormone is the primary regulator of lipolysis during fasting."
Source: PMC3529368 - NCBI

Individuals with underlying GH deficiencies or disruptions, such as those induced by over-fasting, often experience diminished fat loss during prolonged fasts, as impaired GH signaling blunts lipolysis and ketone generation.

This mechanism can also underlie "over-fasting" symptoms observed in some practitioners, where excessive or repeated fasting suppresses GH output. For instance, a recent community report on the Dry Fasting Club Facebook page shared progress photos from an extended fast showing minimal fat reduction, likely indicative of GH suppression from prior over-fasting episodes.

Such suppression may persist long-term post-fast, manifesting as low GH symptoms (e.g., fatigue, reduced recovery, or metabolic slowdown). These can be misattributed to recurring chronic illness, prompting further fasting attempts that exacerbate the cycle, creating a detrimental feedback loop of deepening GH deficiency and stalled progress.

Optimizing Refeeds: Muscle vs. Fat

One of the most critical challenges in the Scorch Protocol is the Refeeding Phase. After a period of "chronic undernutrition" mimicked by deep fasting, the body is desperate to rebuild. However, without the correct hormonal signals, it often prioritizes regaining fat stores over rebuilding expensive lean muscle tissue—a phenomenon known as "post-starvation obesity" or "skinny fat" syndrome.

Bridging the Gap: This is where hGH therapy acts as a force multiplier. It provides the necessary anabolic signal to ensure that the influx of nutrients goes towards hypertrophy (muscle growth) and organ repair rather than adipose (fat) storage.

Evidence: Accelerating Recovery & Composition

A study on aging rats recovering from chronic undernutrition highlighted the stark difference between "Refeeding Alone" and "Refeeding + GH".

  • Without GH: Refeeding alone was slow to restore body mass and simply returned muscles to their baseline levels.
  • With GH: The addition of Growth Hormone restored body mass in just 9 weeks (significantly faster) and resulted in an "overshoot" of muscle growth (hypertrophy), effectively building back better than before.

By using hGH during the refeed, we bridge the gap of lagging natural GH production, ensuring your body rebuilds as a stronger, leaner version of itself.

"Refeeding + GH restored body mass to control levels [within 9 weeks], whereas it was still decreased with refeeding alone."
Source: Ameredes BT et al. (PMID 10573561)
